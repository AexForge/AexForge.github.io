About

Building the plumbing for agents that do real work.

I work at the intersection of large language models and the tools, memory, and guardrails that turn a chat interface into an agent capable of finishing a task end to end — and, in parallel, the delivery discipline that gets that work shipped inside a real organization.

My focus is the Anthropic ecosystem — Claude, MCP, and the patterns that make multi-step agent behaviour reliable rather than fragile. I care less about demos that look impressive once and more about agent systems that hold up under repeat, unglamorous use.

Alongside the technical build, I spend a lot of time on the delivery side of AI adoption: turning experimental agent work into something a program can actually plan, govern, staff, and report on — bridging the gap between "we built a working prototype" and "the business trusts this enough to run on it."

This site is a running log of both halves of that work: shipped tools, delivery frameworks, and the occasional dead end worth documenting.
